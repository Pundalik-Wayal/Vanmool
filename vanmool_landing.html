The Problem

Urban India is getting sicker.
Tribal India is not.

Urban India — 2026

A diet that is destroying the gut

  • Ultra-processed foods stripped of fibre and fermented cultures
  • Rising rates of type 2 diabetes, hypertension, and obesity
  • Disrupted gut microbiome — low diversity, high inflammation
  • No connection to the food traditions that once protected us
  • Health advice that is generic, expensive, and disconnected from roots

Tribal Communities — Same Country

Centuries of metabolic protection

  • Whole grains, forest greens, wild herbs, seasonal eating
  • Markedly lower rates of metabolic and gut-related disease
  • Rich fermented food traditions seeding beneficial gut bacteria
  • Deep plant knowledge with real pharmacological basis
  • A food system shaped by ecology, not profit

The microbiome science is clear. What tribal communities eat — and how they eat — produces measurably different gut profiles than urban populations. Yet this knowledge is disappearing, undocumented, and untranslated for the millions of urban Indians who need it most.

Our Approach

Vanmool translates traditional
food wisdom into modern health.

01

Science-backed awareness

We use microbiome data, multi-omics research, and nutritional science to show urban audiences — clearly and compellingly — what their diet is costing them, and what tribal food traditions offer as an evidence-based alternative.

02

Validated tribal products

A curated product line — tribal grain mixes, wild-herb teas, fermented food starters, and microbiome-validated herbal supplements — ethically sourced from tribal communities, minimally processed, and transparently labelled with the science behind each ingredient.

03

Community & economic impact

Every product creates a dignified, formal economic channel for tribal food producers and foragers — turning ancestral knowledge into livelihoods, while ensuring that the communities whose wisdom built this venture share in its growth.

The Founder

Pundalik Wayal

MSc Biology · Microbiome Researcher · Tribal Community Member

I grew up in a tribal community in Maharashtra, eating forest foods, fermented grains, and herbs that had no labels but had been trusted for centuries. When I went to university and studied gut microbiome science at CSIR-IGIB, I began to see what I had taken for granted through a scientific lens.

The foods of my childhood were shaping gut microbiome diversity. The herbs were modulating immune pathways. The fermented drinks were seeding beneficial bacteria. Meanwhile, the urban diabetic cohort I was studying showed exactly the microbial imbalances that a tribal diet would help prevent. That contrast became Vanmool.

  • MSc Biology (Applied Genomics), Ashoka University
  • Master's Thesis Researcher, Ayurgenomics Unit — CSIR-IGIB, New Delhi
  • Abstract accepted, EMBO-EMBL Symposium on Innate Immunity, Heidelberg
  • Executive Program in Data Science for Social Impact, AshokaX & data.org
  • Government of India Merit Scholarship — Tribal Students Academic Excellence

Our Vision

"The answers to India's metabolic health crisis are not in a laboratory yet to be built — they are in the forests and kitchens of communities that have lived them for generations."

Vanmool exists to make that knowledge visible, credible, and accessible — for the urban millions who need it, and the tribal communities who carry it.
